# Getting Around Dasmariñas City

Dasmariñas City is well-connected by major roads and public transportation. Here's how to get around and reach key destinations.

---

## Major Roads & Highways

| Road                              | Description                                                                    |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Aguinaldo Highway**             | The main arterial road connecting Dasmariñas to Imus, Cavite City, and Manila  |
| **Governor's Drive**              | Links Dasmariñas to Tagaytay City and southern Cavite                          |
| **SLEX (South Luzon Expressway)** | Tollway for faster access to Metro Manila (exit at Susana Heights / Filinvest) |
| **Dasmariñas–Tagaytay Road**      | Scenic route going to Tagaytay City                                            |
| **Dasmariñas Bypass Road**        | Reduces traffic congestion through the city center                             |

---

## Public Transportation

### Jeepney

Jeepneys are the most common mode of transport within Dasmariñas and to nearby cities. Major terminals and routes include:

- **Dasmariñas City Terminal** — located near the city center
- Routes to Imus, Bacoor, Cavite City, Molino, and Tagaytay
- Routes to nearby barangays (Paliparan, Salawag, Sampaloc, Langkaan, etc.)

**Fare:** Starting at ₱12–₱15 for the first 4 km

---

### UV Express / Van for Hire

UV Express vans provide faster transport from Dasmariñas to:

- **Alabang** (Muntinlupa) — a popular route for commuters
- **MOA (Mall of Asia)** via SLEX
- **Buendia / Ayala** (Makati)

**Fare:** Approximately ₱80–₱130 to Metro Manila destinations

---

### Tricycle

Tricycles operate within barangays and short distances. They are commonly used to travel:

- Between subdivisions and main roads
- To markets, schools, and barangay halls

**Fare:** ₱8–₱15 per person for short trips (special trips may cost more)

---

### Ride-Sharing Apps

**Grab** and other ride-sharing services are widely available in Dasmariñas City, making it convenient to travel to key destinations without waiting for public transport.

## Getting to Dasmariñas from Manila

| Mode                     | Route                                           | Travel Time               |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| **Bus**                  | From Buendia / Lawton to Dasmariñas via SLEX    | ~1–2 hours                |
| **UV Express**           | From Alabang or Buendia                         | ~45 min–1.5 hours         |
| **Private Car via SLEX** | Exit at Susana Heights or Aguinaldo Interchange | ~40–60 minutes (off-peak) |

## Tips for Getting Around

- **Peak hours** are 7–9 AM and 5–8 PM — expect heavier traffic along Aguinaldo Highway
- **RFID** is required for vehicles using SLEX toll — load up at any SLEX or NLEX RFID center
- Download **Grab** and **Move It** apps for convenient private transport within the city
- Check local Facebook groups and the **City Government** page for traffic and road closure advisories
