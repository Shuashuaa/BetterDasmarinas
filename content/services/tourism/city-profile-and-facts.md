# Dasmariñas City Profile & Key Facts

## Overview

**Dasmariñas** is a **component city** in the province of **Cavite**, located in the **CALABARZON region (Region IV-A)** of the Philippines. It is the **largest city in Cavite** by both population and land area, and the **wealthiest local government unit** in the province.

## Geography

Dasmariñas is situated approximately **30 kilometers south of Manila** and **10 kilometers from Imus**, the provincial capital of Cavite. The city is well-connected to the Metro Manila area via major roads:

- **Aguinaldo Highway (Gen. Emilio Aguinaldo Highway)**
- **Governor's Drive**
- **Dasmariñas-Tagaytay Road**

The city experiences two pronounced seasons: **wet season (June–October)** and **dry season (November–May)**.

---

## History

Dasmariñas takes its name from **Gomez Pérez Dasmariñas**, the 7th Governor-General of the Philippines during the Spanish colonial period (1590–1593).

Key historical milestones:

- Originally a **barrio of Imus**, Dasmariñas was established as an independent **municipality** in the 19th century
- Rapid growth occurred in the **late 1990s and 2000s** as Metro Manila's urban sprawl extended southward
- On **November 26, 2009**, Republic Act 9723 was signed into law, officially converting the Municipality of Dasmariñas into the **City of Dasmariñas**
- The **Paru-Paro Festival** was launched in 2011 to celebrate the city's second anniversary

## Economy

Dasmariñas is known as the **Industrial Giant of CALABARZON** and is the most economically active city in Cavite.

**Key economic drivers:**

- **Industrial Estates:**
  - First Cavite Industrial Estate (FCIE) — Barangay Langkaan
  - Dasma-Technopark — Paliparan I
  - Molave Compound — Paliparan II

- **Major Commercial Establishments:**
  - SM City Dasmariñas
  - Robinsons Place Dasmariñas
  - WalterMart Dasmariñas
  - Central Mall Dasmariñas

- **Service Sector:** Over 85% of the population are professionals with a high standard of living

---

## Education

Dasmariñas is a major educational hub in CALABARZON, home to several universities and colleges:

- **De La Salle University — Dasmariñas (DLSU-D)**
- **Emilio Aguinaldo College — Cavite**
- **Adventist University of the Philippines** (nearby, in Silang)
- Multiple public high schools and elementary schools across 75 barangays

## Demographics

- **Population:** ~744,511 residents (one of the most populous cities in CALABARZON)
- **Growth:** Rapid population growth driven by urban migration from Metro Manila
- **Subdivisions:** More than **180 subdivisions**
- **Largest Resettlement Area:** Dasmariñas Bagong Bayan (DBB) — one of the largest resettlement communities in the Philippines
