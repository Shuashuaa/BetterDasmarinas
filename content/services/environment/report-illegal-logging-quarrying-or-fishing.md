# Report Illegal Logging, Quarrying, or Fishing

Dasmariñas City enforces environmental laws to protect its remaining green areas, rivers, and natural resources. Residents are encouraged to report any illegal activities threatening the city's environment.

## What Can You Report?

- **Illegal logging** — unauthorized cutting of trees in public or protected areas
- **Illegal quarrying** — unauthorized extraction of sand, gravel, or other materials
- **Illegal fishing** — use of prohibited fishing methods in rivers and inland waterways
- **Illegal dumping** — disposing of waste in waterways, open lots, or unauthorized areas
- **Wildlife poaching** — hunting or capturing protected species

## Information to Provide

When making a report, try to include:

- Location (barangay, street, landmark)
- Date and time of the incident
- Description of the activity
- Number of people involved (if safe to observe)
- Vehicle plate numbers if any (do not put yourself at risk)

## Confidentiality

Reports can be made anonymously. Your identity will be protected upon request.

## Legal Basis

Violations are prosecuted under:

- Republic Act No. 9175 (Chainsaw Act)
- Republic Act No. 7586 (NIPAS Act)
- Republic Act No. 8550 (Philippine Fisheries Code)
- Local environmental ordinances of Dasmariñas City
